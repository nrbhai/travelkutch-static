Significance

Kandla, officially renamed Deendayal Port, is a colossus of maritime trade. Located on the Kandla Creek, it is the largest port in India by volume of cargo handled. It serves as the primary maritime gateway for the entire northern and western hinterland of India, playing a critical role in the nation's economy.

History

The story of Kandla is one of post-independence resilience. After the partition in 1947, India lost the Port of Karachi to Pakistan. To fill this void for western India, Kandla was envisioned and constructed in the 1950s. In 1965, it became Asia's first Special Economic Zone (SEZ). In 2017, it was renamed Deendayal Port Trust to honor Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya.

How to Reach

By Air Kandla Airport (IXY) has daily flights to Mumbai and Ahmedabad.
By Rail Gandhidham Junction (12km) is the main railway hub.
By Road NH-8A connects Kandla directly to Ahmedabad and the rest of India.

Travel Information

Restricted Area The core port area is restricted. Tourists cannot enter without a pass.
Permission Business visitors need passes from the Port Trust authority.
Best for Industrial tourism and logistics enthusiasts.

Best Time to Visit

October to March. The weather is pleasant. Business visits happen year-round.
